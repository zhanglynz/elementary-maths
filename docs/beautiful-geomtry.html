2.1 Plane Geometry

Sum of inner angles of a triangle

We all know that the sum of the three inner angles of a triangle is equal to \(180^0\), but why is this true. Below is a simple proof.

Proof. Observing the picture below, we have \[ \theta_1 + \theta_2 + \theta_3 = 360^0, \] and \[ (\theta_1 + \angle C) + (\theta_2 + \angle A) + (\theta_3 + \angle B) = 540^0. \] Thus, it follows from the above two equations that \[ \angle A + \angle B + \angle C = 540^0 - 360^0=180^0. \] The proof is completed.
